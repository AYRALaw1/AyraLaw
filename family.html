Family & Citizenship

Caring and Dedicated Legal Assistance for Spouse, Parent, Child, and Sibling Visas

Parent Visas

Reuniting with your parents in the United States is a significant milestone. Our knowledgeable staff guide U.S. citizens through the IR-5 and family preference visa application process, ensuring a seamless and successful experience.

Spouse Visas

Both U.S. citizens and lawful permanent residents can petition for their spouses to immigrate to the United States. The right visa choice depends on your specific circumstances, goals, and timeline. Our experienced team will guide you in selecting the best option, ensuring a smooth process from petition preparation to interview readiness.

Child Visas

Reuniting families is a top priority, and our legal team is here to assist in securing the right visa for your children, whether they are biological, adopted, or stepchildren. We provide expert guidance to navigate the immigration process smoothly and efficiently.

Sibling Visa

This visa allows U.S. citizens to petition for their siblings to obtain lawful permanent residency. Our dedicated legal team will assist you through every step of the process, from filing the petition to preparing for the consular interview, ensuring a smooth and stress-free experience. One of the main challenges of this visa is the long wait time due to annual visa limits. We provide expert guidance on understanding the Visa Bulletin and priority dates, keeping you informed and prepared throughout the journey to reunite with your family.

Adoption Visas

Designed for children adopted abroad by U.S. citizens. Child adoption cases in U.S. immigration law can be highly complex due to stringent legal requirements, multiple governing bodies, and the intersection of U.S. immigration law with foreign adoption laws. Adoption-based immigration cases are intricate and require careful planning. Working with an experienced immigration attorney is crucial to navigate the complexities, ensure compliance with all legal requirements, and avoid unnecessary delays or denials.

Our dedicated legal team has a strong track record of successfully refiling and obtaining approvals for adoption cases that were previously denied by USCIS.

Fiancé Visas

Only U.S. citizens can sponsor their fiancé(e) for immigration. To bring your fiancé(e) to the U.S., you’ll need a K-1 visa, which permits entry and requires marriage within 90 days. Our legal team will guide you through the process, helping you gather essential documents and prepare for the consular interview to ensure a smooth and successful application.

Adjustment of Status

Adjustment of status allows eligible individuals already in the U.S. to apply for permanent residency (a green card) without needing to return to their home country. Whether applying through a family-based petition or employment sponsorship, we offer tailored legal support to navigate the process efficiently. Our experienced legal team provides guidance throughout the entire process, ensuring a smooth transition from eligibility assessment to final approval.

Applying for adjustment of status involves a detailed process with multiple forms, documentation requirements, and procedural steps. Once your application is submitted, additional steps may be required, including biometrics collection and a potential interview. We guide you through each stage to ensure you are well-prepared.

Consular Processing

Consular processing is the procedure for individuals outside the U.S. to apply for an immigrant visa at a U.S. Embassy or Consulate. Whether you are seeking a nonimmigrant visa for temporary stay or an immigrant visa leading to a green card, you must apply through the appropriate U.S. consular office. This process differs from adjustment of status, which applies to those applying for permanent residency from within the U.S.

Before beginning consular processing, you must have an approved immigrant petition. During this process, our team ensures accurate and complete petition preparation and filing.

Once approved, your petition is forwarded to the National Visa Center (NVC), during which we guide you through NVC requirements, including fee payments, document submission, and additional forms, ensuring a smooth process.

Once your consular interview is successfully completed, there are essential steps to take before entering the U.S. as a lawful permanent resident, which we guide you through to ensure a smooth transition to the U.S.

Citizenship

Naturalization is the process through which a lawful permanent resident (green card holder) can obtain U.S. citizenship. Our team offers expert legal guidance to help you navigate eligibility requirements, prepare your application, and successfully complete the naturalization journey.

Depending on the reason for inadmissibility, different waivers may be available, such as the I-601, I-601A, and I-212 waivers. Our team helps you determine the most suitable waiver for your situation and guides you through the application process.

Inadmissibility Waivers

Inadmissibility refers to specific conditions or legal factors that can prevent a person from entering the U.S. or obtaining lawful permanent resident status. These factors may include health-related issues, criminal offenses, immigration fraud, unlawful presence, and other legal barriers.

Depending on the reason for inadmissibility, different waivers may be available, such as the I-601, I-601A, and I-212 waivers. Our team helps you determine the most suitable waiver for your situation and guides you through the application process.
